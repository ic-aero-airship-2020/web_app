Project Info

What is the Group Design Project?

The Group Design Project (GDP) is an integral part of the Aeronautical Engineering MEng course. Working in groups of about 25, students are given a brief and 5 weeks to deliver a project. They are aided by a panel of academic supervisors from the Aeronautics department to whom they present in a weekly debrief.

Autonomous Airship 2020

This GDP group was made up of 25 students split into 6 subteams. We worked together over 5 weeks to create an initial design from scratch. Take a look a the Project Timeline section below to find out more about our design process.

Project Timeline

Week 1

- We set up the configeration layout with a GNVR profile and a mission profile

- We could carry 200g of mass with a 2 motor configeration

- A Raspberry Pi with a web-based GUI and ground station was considered for communication

- For navigation, Computer vision and SLAM would be utilised

Week 2

- Specifics of the possible mission were laid out

- The envelope shape was changed to 1.5 m to allow for a weight margin of 1500 g

- Sensors and an IR camera was added to improve functionality

- A 3 motor configeration was finalised as the most efficient and stable configeration

Week 3

- Envelope was shrunk to 1.1 m

- Raspberry Pi Zero was finalised as the final onboard CPU

- Voice commands via NLP was implemented in the web-app and onboard microphone

- Speakers and LED strips were added to the payload to increase interactivity

Week 4

- On-board microphone was scrapped for its unsuitability to the missions

- Remote control via the web app was added

- LQR based control law for stability was put in place

Week 5

- Navigation was refined using visual odometry

- Control was improved thorugh trajectory shaping

- Autonomous charging station design was finalised
